# How to Obtain a WashU Guest Key

## Overview
External collaborators who need access to CNDA must first obtain a WashU Guest Key. This is Washington University's network credential for non-WashU personnel and is required before you can create a CNDA2 account. WashU Keys are issued through a faculty sponsorship process and are not managed by the CNDA Help Desk.

---

## Before You Start
- You must have a WashU faculty member who will sponsor your application
- This is typically the PI on the project or projects you are working on
- You only need one sponsor, regardless of how many projects you are associated with

---

## Step 1: Complete the Guest Request Form
Complete the WashU Key guest request form:
- [WashU Key Guest Request Form](https://connect.wustl.edu/guest/guestrequest/)

Use the following values when completing the form:

- **Page 1:** Enter your demographic information
- **Page 2:**
  - Duration: **6–12 months**
  - Visiting location: **N/A**
  - Purpose: **Electronic access to cnda.wustl.edu**
- **Page 3:** Enter the email address of your WashU faculty sponsor

---

## Step 2: Wait for Approval
- You will receive an email notification when your key is approved
- You will also receive a notification when your key is approaching expiration

::: tip
- Keys are valid for the duration selected during the application, up to a maximum of one year
- Contact your faculty sponsor to request a renewal before your key expires
- CNDA Help Desk does not manage WashU Keys and cannot approve, renew, or revoke them
:::

---

## Information for Sponsors
If you are a WashU faculty member sponsoring an external collaborator:

- You can contact WU IT to revoke a key early if the collaboration ends
- Keys are valid for the duration selected during the application, up to a maximum of one year, and must be renewed by the sponsor
- CNDA Help Desk does not manage WashU Keys
